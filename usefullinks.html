    • Galvanization

    Galvanization is the process of applying a protective zinc coating to steel or iron to prevent rusting and corrosion. The most common method is hot-dip galvanizing , where the metal is dipped into molten zinc.

    Purpose :

    Protects iron or steel from oxidation (rust). Increases durability and lifespan of metal structures. Cost-effective way to prevent corrosion.

    How It Works :

    Zinc acts as a sacrificial anode , meaning it corrodes preferentially, protecting the underlying steel or iron. Even if the coating is slightly damaged, the zinc will still protect the exposed area.

    Common Methods :
    Hot-dip Galvanizing

    Immersing metal in molten zinc.

    Electro-Galvanizing

    Using electroplating to deposit a layer of zinc.

    Spraying

    Applying zinc coating via thermal spraying.

    Why Galvanize?

    Main Reasons to Galvanize Metal (Usually Steel or Iron):

    • Prevent Rust and Corrosion
      • Iron and steel are prone to oxidation (rust) when exposed to water and oxygen.
      • Galvanizing protects the metal with zinc, acting as a barrier and sacrificial anode.
    • Prolong Lifespan of Structures
      • Galvanized coatings can last 50+ years in most conditions.
      • Common applications include fences, utility poles, pipes, and bridges.
    • Cost-Effective Protection
      • Galvanizing is more affordable than frequent repairs or replacements due to rust.
    • Low Maintenance
      • Once galvanized, metal requires little or no maintenance â€” even in tough environments.
    • Environmentally Resilient
      • Ideal for coastal areas, industrial zones, and freezing or damp conditions.
    • Self-Healing Around Scratches
      • Zinc continues to protect exposed steel through cathodic protection, even if scratched.

    Sector Applications
    Building Beams, scaffolding, fencing
    Utilities Power towers, street lighting
    Farming Barn hardware, water troughs
    Transportation Guardrails, auto parts
